PROFESSIONAL

Pathways U Reflection

For my Pathway U assessment, the result surprisingly matched my values and ideals, although there were some parts where it didn’t match me too well; overall, it did a pretty good job. For interest, my primary career interests are ‘Enterprising’ and ‘Conventional.’ For my values, I got ‘Achievement’ and ‘Independence.’ For personality, my highest scores are: ‘Open to Experience,’ ‘Conscientiousness,’ and ‘Honesty.’ Lastly, my workplace preferences are ‘Recognition’ and ‘Collaboration.’

As I mentioned previously, my results are pretty accurate. Enterprising means go-getter, leading people, decision maker and lead and action-oriented. I often find myself leading group projects and making group decisions a lot which reflected that pretty well. For conventional, it means detailed oriented and procedure. I would say I like a set of guidelines, but I can always work around problems and accommodate when need to.

As I mention in the ‘Missions’ tab, personal growth and achievement are my personal values. Sense of accomplishment is rewarding as you have a sense of accomplishment. Independence: I like to also work at my own pace as well in my own space where I have everything I need for workflow.

I’m always open to trying new things. Like this past year, I started snowboarding and rock climbing, and who knows what else to come. Conscientious, my peers also see me as organized and disciplined. The test also reported me as honest. I know people also see me as genuine, but hopefully, that carries to the new people I meet.

Last but not least is workplace preference. I love recognition. Whether recognizing others or getting recognized, it is always nice to hear words of affirmation in and outside the workplace.

As someone still uncertain about my career path, these results will help me dive deeper into what I really want to do as a person.
