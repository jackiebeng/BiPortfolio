FIRST YEAR REFLECTION

BrotherHood Initiative Reflection

I remember when I first got the invitation to sign up for the Bi around May of 2022, I thought to myself, what even is this? Until I heard some of my high school friends signing up, I thought this might be a great way to meet people and grow connections. Who would’ve thought that the Bi has made such a significant impact on my first year here at UW.

So what does Bi mean to me? Bi means a sense of community for underrepresented men of color at UW. Bi means nothing like its name, which sounds like a frat or Greek life. Bi also means a place to get help to navigate UW as well as opportunities to meet previous cohorts all the way to the CEO of a billion-dollar company.

To me, Bi has honestly made up a good chunk of my first year at UW. I would honestly feel even more lonely if I wasn’t in Bi. Bi has brought me a sense of community and a community full of people of color. Which also seg-ways towards how Bi is important for learning. For starters, I really appreciate the faculty constantly checking in with you to see how you are doing personally and academically. I feel like these small things have a significant impact on learning as you kinda have a sense of someone caring for your academic success. But not only that, but having a support system and a team to go to when you're struggling in any part of UW is nice. I know for sure that when I have problems with my schedule and financial aid, I will be at Paul’s office because he just knows what to do.

What advice would I give to future Bi scholars to make the most out of their Bi experience? Go to events, get to know the faculty as if they are your friends, and chill around at The Brooklyn Trails building; not only will you meet other members, but you also have the chance to hang out with faculty. And don’t be afraid to go to them; the only thing they want from you is to succeed!
